Austria

The Austrian Development Agency GmbH (ADA) is the operational unit of Austrian Development Cooperation(ADC) and acts as a hub for the overall bilateral cooperation in Georgia. ADC supports development programs and projects targeting poverty reduction, peace-building and environment protection. Furthermore, other actors like the Austrian Development Bank and the ÖKB of the Ministry of Finance are the major actors in the field of Softloans. In addition, different Austrian line ministries are active with cooperation projects, like Foreign Affairs, Agriculture and Environment, Interior or Defence.

The current bilateral Country Strategy 2012 – 2020 (mid-term reviewed in 2017) is oriented towards the support of Georgia in its economic and democratic transition process and its efforts to align with European structures, standards and values:

  • Agriculture and Rural Development: Raising productivity through the creation of local and international value chains, the modernization of planting methods, improved marketing, the diversification of products, communal farming through the organization of smallholders in agricultural associations, an improvement of product quality through adequate plant and animal protection measures, and the certification of and transition to ecologically sustainable biological planting methods are the areas of primary focus in the agricultural sector. Building capacities of Public Institutions on national and municipal level in the field of Agriculture is another strong aspect of the assistance package provided to the Government of Georgia.
  • Austria’s activities in Forestry are guided by vast experience and expertise the country has. It supports Georgia’s national legal reforms, the strengthening of administrative capacities (e.g. forestry inspection) on the local, regional and national level, as well as the promotion of policy dialogue between local government, civil society and the private sector. Bio-diversity and anti-corruption measures are other important areas of ADA’s assistance in the forestry sector.
  • ADA’s interventions in Decentralization, Regional Development, Good Governance, gender mainstreaming, strengthening of civil society and the private sector are driven not only by the aim to further support the democratization process of Georgia, but also to ensure improvement of the investment climate and the overall economic condition in the country.

Most common funding mechanisms

  • Technical Assistance
  • Grants
  • Co-funding (Grants)
  • Business Partnerships (up to 50% Grant for investing companies)
  • Softloans (OeKB)
  • Multilateral Contributions (EU, UN, International Financial Institutions)

Strategic development areas include:

  • Economic Growth, in particular Agruculture and Rural Development
  • Good Governance, with a focus on Local and Regional Development
  • Sustainable Use of National Resources

Agriculture, Forestry and Rural Development are anticipated to remain key-sectors in ADA’s assistance. Regional projects (including Armenia in the South Caucasus) will remain a priority for ADC. Gender Equality plays a special role to rural development, to strengthen legal position of women and improve their living conditions and local government reform.

Bulgaria

The Bulgarian Government has been providing ODA since 2013. Assistance to Georgia is based on following documents:

  • Council of Ministers’ decree 234 of 1 August 2011 about the Republic of Bulgaria’s participation in international development cooperation;
  • Mid-term program of the Republic of Bulgaria about development cooperation and humanitarian aid 2016-2019;
  • New European Consensus on Development - 'Our world, our dignity, our future' (2017/C210/01)

Thereof, Georgia, as a Black Sea country, is one of the geographic priorities of Bulgarian ODA. Thematic priorities of Bulgarian ODA to Georgia are the following:

  • Enhancing the quality of education;
  • Sustainable management in agriculture and foods;
  • Support for democratic and responsible institutions;
  • Human rights

Most common funding mechanisms

  • Technical Assistance
  • Grant

In 2017, the Embassy of the Republic of Bulgaria completed following projects:

  • “Strengthening Cоmmunity Care and Inclusive Education for Children with Disabilities in Georgia” (in cooperation with UNICEF);
  • “Capacity building of the Public Defender’s Office in Kakheti Region” (in cooperation with the Public Defender’s Office);
  • - “Strengthening the capacity of the National Food Agency of Georgia for risk assessment and risk management” (in cooperation with the National Food Agency of Georgia and in the frames of the US Emerging Donors Challenge Fund)

In 2017, the Embassy of the Republic of Bulgaria started implementation of following projects which are ongoing in 2018:

  • “Capacity building of Public Defender`s office (PDO) in Kakheti region – continuation” (in cooperation with the Public Defender’s Office);
  • “Monitoring Conditions of Juvenile and Female Prisoners in Georgian Penitentiary” (in cooperation with the Human Rights Center);
  • “Trial Monitoring with Focus on women” (in cooperation with the Human Rights Center);
  • “Complementary Rehabilitation and Re-socialisation Support in the Georgian Penitentiary System” (in cooperation with the Human Rights Center);
  • “Young Leaders for Sustainable Development Goals” (in cooperation with the Children and Youth National Center);
  • “Multicultural Educational Center School ‘Momavali’” (in cooperation with International Educational Center);

The Government of the Republic of Bulgaria intends to continue ODA to Georgia with following focus: support for qualitative, comprehensive and modern education; support for agriculture development and harmonization with EU requirements; support for capacity building and development in the area of democratization, good governance and human rights.

Czech Republic

Georgia is on the highest rank of partner countries for Czech Development Cooperation as well as for the Transition Promotion Programme. The Czech Development Cooperation with Georgia is regulated by governmental strategy paper efficient till 2023. Development projects are managed by the Czech Development Agency (CzDA), its implementing partners and partly by the Czech Embassy. Transition programme is managed by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. A separate programme is Government Scholarships.

Most common funding mechanisms

  • Grant
  • Technical Assistance
  • Scholarships
  • Investment projects

Strategic development areas include:

  • Inclusive Social Development (Health Care, Social Welfare)
  • Rural development
  • Democracy and Good Governance

Czech Republic focuses on assistance to Georgia with transposition of EU directives for prevention of industrial disasters and handling of chemical substances. Technical assistance with establishment of Phytosanitary Surveillance Monitoring System is the second direction of the Czech support of the Association Agenda.

Health care and Social Welfare belong to long term priorities of Czech Development Cooperation for Georgia. It includes prevention, early diagnosis and treatment of cancer and enhancing efficiency of Social Welfare System.

Czech Development Cooperation also targets mountainous regions of Georgia. Its priority areas are Tusheti, Khevsureti, Pshavi and Gudamakari. In these areas the Czech Republic promotes sustainable economic development either through support of efficiency of traditional agriculture or through support of sustainable tourism development. The Czech Republic`s engagement also includes capacity building of local municipalities in appropriate management of natural resources, high level of biodiversity and culture landscape character. Another focus in these areas is accessibility of health care, education and disaster prevention. A separate activity is support of beekeeping in several Georgian regions.

Human rights and strengthening of civil society are top priorities for Transition Promotion Programme in general and particularly in occupied territories of Georgia.

Estonia

Estonia's Development Cooperation is a basic outlet for sharing its reform experiences with transition economy countries. Georgia is a priority country for Estonia (along with Moldova and Ukraine). The Undersecretary of External Economic Policy and Development Cooperation Affairs (Division of Development Cooperation and Humanitarian Assistance) of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs coordinates Estonia’s development cooperation programmes. The basis of cooperation in 2016–2018 will be the Estonian development cooperation country strategy for Georgia, which reflects the needs and development strategies of Georgia and determines the development directions and areas where Estonia can offer value added. Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Estonia is currently undergoing internal preparations for the next development cooperation country strategy paper for Georgia 2018-2020. Total ongoing aid of Estonia to Georgia amounts to 2.5 million GEL (1.1 million USD).

The Embassy also manages a small microfinance program. In 2015, 40 thousand EUR was provided to finance three projects under the program.

Most common funding mechanisms

  • Technical Assistance
  • Grant

Strengthening democratic state structures and systems

  • Activities contributing to the implementation of the EU Association Agreement and the Reform Programme and the strengthening of administrative capability and transparency of state institutions by the utilisation of e-governance and information and communication technologies (ICT) in public administration,
  • Involving civil society in governance and formation of reform processes, improving participatory democracy and capability of participating in good governance; improving outreach regarding the EU.

Supporting entrepreneurial development

  • Supporting the development of small enterprises, including supporting the building of startups,
  • Supporting youth and small enterprises in rural areas, in particular, by consulting on how to improve product quality to comply with the EU Free Trade Agreement standards.

The horizontal themes addressed by Estonia’s development cooperation in Georgia are:

  • human rights, gender equality, environmental sustainability, ICT, and internally displaced persons. The horizontal themes may also be regarded as separate cooperation goals.

It is expected that the same sectors will remain priority sectors for Estonian aid. Estonia intends to provide support in the implementation of the Association Agreement.
